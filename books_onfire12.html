THE SUN'S BAPTISM

Zara Chabby, seasoned as a well-worn saddle, his skin
baked to terracotta

By countless desert suns,

Squinted at the horizon.
Solitude, his silent
mistress,

Trailed him like a wraith
across the
sun-bleached dunes.

He craved her embrace,

The whispering emptiness that cleansed the soul like monsoon winds.

His camel, Ishmael, a shaggy relic of countless treks,
snorted,

Kicking up a dusty halo.
"Patience, old friend," Zara rasped,

His voice a dry rustle against the endless silence. Days bled

Into each other, marked
only by the

Sun's fiery chariot
ascending

And descending the
velvet sky.

But today, the desert held a different breath. A shimmer

On the horizon, a mirage birthed from longing,
or perhaps

Something more. As Zara ventured closer, the
shimmering mirage

Solidified into a palm tree, its fronds swaying in a
breeze he

Couldn't feel. Beneath it, a well gleamed
like a fallen star,

Promise glittering
in its depths.

Hesitation, a rare desert
flower, bloomed in
Zara's chest.

Was this an oasis, a cruel
jest of sun and thirst,
or something else entirely?

Curiosity, that restless
desert wind,
tugged at his beard.

He dis-mounted, the sand
singing beneath his
calloused feet.

Kneeling at the well's edge, Zara peered into the
inky black water.

His reflection, a map of sun-etched wrinkles,
stared back.

He dipped his weathered hands, the coolness a shock against his sun-baked skin.

As he brought them up, a single golden coin glinted between his fingers.

No ordinary coin. Etched on its surface was a swirling galaxy,

Stars dancing in miniature. A gasp es-caped Zara's lips.

He'd seen these symbols before, whispered in campfire tales by nomads under

Moonlit skies. They spoke of the Starfall Gate, a hidden portal said to

lead to other worlds, other lives.

The coin, heavy with possibility, warmed in his palm.

Was this the solitude he craved, not an escape from this world.

But a journey beyond it? Fear and excitement wrestled within him.

Two vultures circling a carcass. He glanced at Ishmael.

The beast's wise eyes seeming to urge him onwards.

With a muttered prayer to the sun, Zara tossed the coin into the well.

The water erupted, a silver geyser spraying him with liquid light.

His heart hammered against his ribs as the well's darkness yawned open.

Revealing not stars, but swirling sand.

He plunged in, the sand swallowing him whole.

The world dissolved into a whirlwind of grit and wind,

Then reformed into a sunlit cave, eerily cool despite the

Desert heat. A woman, eyes like pools of molten gold, sat

Cross-legged on a woven sand rug.

Mystery lady :

"Welcome, Zara Chabby,
you have sought soli-tude, but found a riddle."

she said, her voice like the chiming of desert bells.

Zara Chabby:

( Zara stared, tongue dry.)

"Who are you? Where am I?"

Mystery lady :

(A smile curling her lips)

"I am the Keeper of Crossroads,"

"This is the heart of the Sun's Furnace, where time is molten and realities bend."

She pointed to a tapestry woven from sand, depicting figures from forgotten tales, walking hand-in-hand with beings from galaxies unknown.

Mystery lady :

"Your coin held the key, your thirst the trigger. Now, choose your path."

Choices, a viper s nest, lay before Zara. He could wander the tapestry, stepping into lives untold, experiencing a thousand suns in a single breath. Or, he could return to his desert, solitude clinging to him like a desert cloak.
But before he could choose, the cave trembled. A guttural roar echoed through the sand, shaking the very bones of the earth. The woman's golden eyes flared.

Mystery lady :

"He comes," she hissed. "The Devourer of Sands."

A creature of shadows and dust materialized from the cave's depths, its form shifting like desert mi-rages. Fear, a long-dormant beast, awoke in Zara's chest. But then, he saw its eyes, filled with the same desperate emptiness he had sought to escape.

Mystrey lady:

"It seeks not your life," the Keeper whispered, "but your solitude. Your path, Zara, lies not in escaping the world, but in sharing its burden."

Understanding, sudden and sharp, pierced Zara's heart. He had misunderstood solitude. It wasn't an escape, but a responsibility, a wellspring of empathy for the countless souls lost in their own deserts. He turned to the Devourer, not with fear, but with outstretched hands.

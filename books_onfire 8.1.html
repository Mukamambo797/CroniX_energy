BOOK II


CHAPTER IV

HARD SONG

In starlit silence, beneath moonlit eaves,

Where shadows curled and whispered secrets weave,

Zara-Chabby tossed, a soul adrift, Sleep's gentle touch an elusive gift.

He yearned for slumber, for oblivion's embrace,

To escape the visions that haunted his face,

A tapestry woven with terrors untold,

Of serpents that coiled and whispers of cold.

He cursed the night, its star-studded hold,

And cried, "O Rest, why do you withhold Your silken touch,

your balm for the weary brain? Why must I wander this desolate plane?"

But a voice, ethereal, arose from the dark,

A wisp of wind, on embers of a spark,

"Mortal," it sighed, "your slumber you seek,

Yet within your heart, a legend lies meek."

Zara, startled, sat upright in bed,

His eyes wide with wonder, the words in his head.

"Legend?" he breathed,

"I, a simple man, What tales of grandeur could my life span?"

The voice chuckled, a melody strange,

"Within your sorrow, a warrior's rage,

A fire unlit, a story untold,

Of trials faced, and hearts made bold."

And then, like a moth drawn to the moon's soft glow,

Zara felt memories rise, a long-forgotten flow,

Of journeys perilous, through deserts unfurled,

Of beasts he'd battled, and wisdom unfurled.

He saw mountains climbed, and oceans crossed,

The secrets of stars, at great cost,

He saw lovers lost, and friendships forged anew,

The tapestry of his life, vibrant and true.

Each thread, a hardship, a triumph, a tear,

Woven together, facing every fear,

And as he saw himself, with newfound sight,

A hero unmasked, bathed in moonlit light,

A song burst forth, a ballad untold,

Of battles won, and hearts made bold,

Of love and loss, and laughter's refrain,

The symphony of his life, echoing in the rain.

He sang of the sun, and the moon's gentle grace,

Of stardust in his eyes, and sorrow's embrace,

And with each note, his fear melted away,

Replaced by the fire of a glorious day.

When the song faded, and dawn kissed the skies,

Sleep held him gently, with tears in his eyes,

For he knew then, sleep was not just rest,

But a canvas to paint, where legends are blessed.

So now, when slumber eludes Zara-Chabby's eye,

He closes his lids, and lets the stories fly,

For within his dreams, a legend ignites,

A symphony woven with darkness and light.

And if you find yourself restless, beneath the moon's gaze,

Close your eyes, and listen,

for Zara-Chabby's song weaves through the maze,

The tale of a hero, etched in the night,

The legend untold, bathed in starlight.

Thus sang Zara Chabby,
